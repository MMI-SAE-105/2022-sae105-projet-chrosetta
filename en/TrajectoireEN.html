The optimistic sound of Nepal
Trajectoire

Buddhist philosophy found in pop culture

Through his music, Nepal portrays a vision of happiness that is based on inner peace and wisdom. Values that are well and truly present in the Buddhist philosophy. In this sense, the author Hermann Hesse is a great influence for the rapper Nepal. He makes many references to this author in his sounds. First of all in “Sundance”, “J’droppe ça par étape comme dans l’bouquin d’Hermann Hesse”. Ici, “par étape”, refers to the different stages that Siddhartha goes through in the eponymous work of Hermann Hesse. Then, in the musical clip of the sound “éveil”, we see the cover of the book.

Finally, Nekfeu in the song “n’oublies pas” in tribute to Nepal. He writes “j’ai lu tous les livres d’Hermann Hesse, l’impression de mieux comprendre tes textes”.

The sound Trajectory

Other songs of Nepal convey this message. That of accepting things as they are, of knowing how to appreciate them and of learning to move forward despite everything. As shown by his song Trajectory.

This track is the fourth track of the album of Adios Bahamas. Album released on January 10, 2020. Nepal is a rapper and beatmaker. He started in the 2010s. He became a figure of the underground scene.

A lesson of life

The trajectory sound brings many lessons. First of all, it expresses the need to always move forward and stay the same despite the various trials. As the sentence "Humility and strength to go" shows us. Second, a value found in Siddhartha, the need to think for oneself. "Rather die truly than reason like a damn zombie". Positivity is the most important value conveyed in this title. "We must not forget the magic in our irises, Don't let yourself be disarmed, reality, you create it in part". A message of hope for his listeners. The notion of positivity is reinforced by Nassim Haramein's speech at the end of the track. "It doesn't help, you have to stay positive, by remaining aware of what's going on without ignoring it. You have to have this joy of living, this openness, this positivity, because negativity destroys itself. »
