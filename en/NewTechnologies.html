New technologies
a hope for equality ?

Thanks to the evolution of technology, and more specifically the robotics sector, new innovations are improving the daily lives of many people. Thanks to these innovations, disabled people could in the future evolve in the same way as people without disabilities. Their disabilities will no longer be an obstacle to their progress.

Innovations for people with disabilities

There are already many inventions to improve the daily life of disabled people.

The all-purpose wheelchair

The invention of this wheelchair allows people with reduced mobility to be more independent.

The reading ring

This ring reads out loud the written content. It allows visually impaired people to enjoy a book in handwriting. This object increases and facilitates access to information for visually impaired people.

The future promises an end to inequalities

In the near future, innovations more developed than the previous ones will allow to reach equity. Many researchers aim to develop alternatives to erase the differences created by disabilities.

The exoskeleton

Many exoskeletons are already on the market. However, the complete device for a person who cannot stand upright is not yet available. This device brings many hopes. It would ideally allow a person with a motor disability to walk.

Bionic eyes

Bionic eyes consist in the fixation of an implant under the retina which would allow the eye to capture light signals. It would send stimuli to the retinal neurons. Thus, the visually impaired, blind by disease or accident could regain their sight.
