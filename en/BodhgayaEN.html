Bodhgaya
Holy place of Buddhism

Bodhgaya : an oriental symbol

In the Eastern culture, more precisely in Buddhism, Bodhgaya is considered as the "navel" of the world. This city is a major place in the history of this religion. It is in this place, in the 5th century before our era, that the prince Siddhartha Gautama reached enlightenment. Thus he became Buddha. This event marks the birth of Buddhism.

Its geographical location

Bodhgaya is located in the North-East of India. In the province of Bihar.

Buddhism : a religion, a philosophy ?

When we talk about Buddhism, we generally associate it with a religion. However, is it really a religion in its strictest sense? Unlike Western religions, Buddhism does not worship a god, creator of the universe, to whom one must be saved. Buddhism offers teachings of life. Each man, according to his will, follows them or not, in order to reach his vision of salvation. The followers of this religion do not pray to a god to show their devotion. But rather to show respect for the wisdom of that man.

Major places of Bodhgaya

In order to get to know Bodhgaya better, here are the different places that make this city a major place

The Bodhi Tree

The Bodhi tree is the one under which Prince Siddhartha stayed more than a month in meditation. At the end of this time, he reached enlightenment. It was under the Bodhi tree that he became Buddha. At the very spot where he was, Emperor Ashoka built the diamond throne.

The Mahabodhi Temple

The Mahabodhi Temple is the main place in the city of Bodhgaya. It is built around the Bodhi tree. This makes it a major place of pilgrimage. It is in the 7th century that this temple was built. Since 2002, it has been entered, by UNESCO, in the sites of the world heritage.

The Statue of the Great Buddha

The statue of the great Buddha is not only a major place for the religious aspect, but also for the tourist aspect. Its construction dates from 1989, by the XIV Dalai Lama. It represents the Buddha in meditation activity. It measures, in total, 24 meters in height.

The 3 places of pilgrimages of Buddhism

Lumbini

Prince Siddhartha Gautama was born in the village of Lumbini. It is located in Nepal, near the border of India.

Sarnath

Sarnath représente l'endroit où Bouddha donna son premier enseignement. Enseignement connu sous le nom du “Sermon de la Roue de Dharma".

Sarnath represents the place where Buddha gave his first teaching. Teaching known under the name of the "Sermon of the Wheel of Dharma". Buddha exposes the four noble truths (Dukkha; Samudaya; Nirodha; Magga). This event took place two months after his enlightenment.

Kushinagar

It was in Kushinagar that Buddha spoke his last words and entered Parinirvana.
