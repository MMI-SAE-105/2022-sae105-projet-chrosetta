Toxic Positivity

Disclaimer: I am not talking about all personal development programs. Only in the last decades, many books have been published that do not respect human constraints.

The dictatorship of personal development

This culture of personal development is omnipresent. It is manifested through books, videos or even posts on social networks. Personal development spreads this ideal of the perfect human being, ultra productive, always motivated, fulfilled, etc. The perfect human being becomes, thus, a goal to reach. And, in order to reach this goal, all forms of defects must be denied. This is where it becomes problematic. The human being is not perfect, he has limits, weaknesses. He cannot become a perfect being. That's why, a big part of his books is not realistic. They seek to establish a vision of the man which is not realizable.

The guilt engendered

The high expectations of his books lead to disappointment among the readers. People who follow his programs are often looking for improvement. However, with these unattainable goals, consumers blame themselves. Performance becomes the watchword of personal development. If the one who wants to apply it can't do it, then he is too "weak" to succeed. From then on, what is supposed to help you, ends up undermining you.

Learning to accept

In reality, his books are not bad. On the contrary, they can provide ways to approach things differently. But you have to know how to put things into perspective and take a step back from what you say. Flaws are human. We must learn to accept them and not feel guilty about them. Just like negative emotions. They have their roles to play in our way of living things. It is not human to be at 100% all the time.
